Frequently Asked Questions


What is the OTL?

OTL stands for Open Token Lending. Right now we are in the alpha version of a proposed uniswap style fully decentralized and censorship resistant token lending protocol in which any token can be lent against any other token as collateral.

How do I interact with the OTL?

You can connect via metamask to the OTL and supply DAI, USDC and USDT to the platform and start earning interest immediately. You may withdraw your deployed tokens at any time, including accrued interest (provided there is liquidity, which we are working towards improving).

In due course we will be adding support for other wallets such as Ledger, Trezor etc as well.

What can I lend with?

While eventually it should be possible to lend any token against any token, right now we allow DAI, USDC and USDT. More currency pegged tokens will be added in the coming days on the lending side.

Who can borrow from the platform? What is being used as collateral?

Eventually anyone with tokenised assets can borrow from the platform by using the asset as collateral. Right now we have tokenized a commercial property loan in Victoria, Australia and are using that as collateral. You can check details of the property and the loan from the Asset Explorer. To ensure a good amount of over collateralization for lenders we have picked a loan which is only lending to 60% of the property’s value. On top of that, we have a collateral factor of 80% of the value of the loan on the system.

What is the Asset Explorer?

The asset explorer is where you can view the tokenized assets being used as collateral on the system. It includes the token name, the type of asset, it’s collateral value on the system, and the link to the token contract itself. By clicking the link under asset Type, you will be directed to an information page with more details on the asset including any relevant loan agreements.


How often is interest calculated? Can I take my deployed funds out any time?

Interest is calculated per each ethererum block which is roughly every 15 seconds. You can take out your funds, including interest, anytime - provided there is enough liquidity in the pool. Liquidity of a particular asset is shown when you click the asset. In the example below, it shows that 10 USDT can be withdrawn from the pool.


What is the road map for allowing other tokens as collateral?

We have created an admin module via which new tokens can be added as collateral. You can check it here at https://app.konkrete.finance/admin

As soon as initial testing is complete, we will be opening up the new token addition to be done via a community vote.

What is the expected process for adding new collateral?

We are planning to distribute platform tokens which would entitle its holders to any fees generated from the platform and the right to vote on any decisions about the platform.

Any token holder will be able to put up executable code as a proposal which would be put up to community vote and if approved will lead to updates to the platform.

Adding new tokens as collateral would follow the same process.

Eventually we expect the need for an admin signed off process to be eliminated with an architecture that allows any token to be borrowed against any token.

Where can I find the platform tokens?

Once the alpha is complete, platform tokens will be distributed to investors, advisors, core team and to the community. Stay tuned for updates.

Is the OTL meant to allow real world tokens as collateral?

The OTL is agnostic of what kinds of assets are represented via the tokens. It is meant to be a decentralized and censorship resistant lending protocol. What assets are added to it are expected to be determined by the community once the alpha is complete and soon after that even those permissions will not be needed.

In terms of how real world asset tokenization can be achieved and our thoughts around it please refer to this article.

Note that the OTL and the real world asset tokenization efforts are independent of each other. Composability makes it possible for them to talk to each other.

What are kTokens?

kTokens are similar to cTokens, or aTokens and iTokens etc from other money market platforms out there. They represent your claim to the money you have deployed on the platform and its interest.

Once you deploy DAI, USDC, USDT to the platform you will receive an ERC20 kDAI, kUSDC, kUSDT respectively. These are interest bearing tokens and steadily rise in value.

While on the platform interface they are not visible, you may check them in your metamask by adding those custom tokens.

Welcome to the OTL, a decentralized lending protocol that allows lending of any ERC20 token against any other ERC20 token as collateral.

You can start lending with USDC, DAI, USDT, and ADR, and earn interest on it right away.

To get started simply connect with MetaMask. If you don’t have MetaMask, you can download it from https://metamask.io/

Once connected to MetaMask, your screen will look like something above. On left hand side you can see all the ERC20 tokens you can lend with, their current lending rates and how much you have deployed vs left in your wallet.

On the right hand side you can see all the ERC20 tokens currently being used as collateral with its details via the blue links.

At the top of the page you can see the total collateral on the market, the total loans utilised on market, your total deployed balance, as well as your current overall rate of return.

The interest rate changes dynamically based on supply and demand as people deploy to and withdraw from the pool.

To deploy, simply select the token you wish to deploy by clicking on it.

Enable the token. A prompt will come up on MetaMask asking for your confirmation. Once confirmed, the page will refresh and should look like the above.
Enter the amount you wish to deploy and press Deploy. Your money starts earning interest immediately!

To withdraw, simply hit the withdraw button with your desired amount. Given enough liquidity, the tokens will flow back to your wallet.

That’s it!
Interest will start accruing every 15 seconds or so.
