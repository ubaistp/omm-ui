Frequently Asked Questions


What is the OPM?

Open Market (OPM) is a censorship resistant money market protocol where lenders can get a consistent rate of return in a permissionless, trust minimized manner.

How do I interact with the OPM?

You can connect via metamask or wallet connect to the OPM and supply DAI (and soon oUSD) to the platform to start earning interest immediately. You may withdraw your deployed tokens at any time, including accrued interest (provided there is liquidity, which we are working towards improving).

What can I lend with?

Currently you can lend with DAI. Support for other currencies such as oUSD/USDT/USDC will be added soon.

Who can borrow from the platform? What is being used as collateral?

Eventually anyone with accepted assets (including tokenised real world assets) can borrow from the platform by using the asset as collateral. For now, during the beta, we (the OpenDAO) are the only borrowers on the system.

What is being used as collateral?

We currently have two types of collateral on the OPM during the beta:

     1. Custodial Liquidity Pool Tokens (LPT) representing a share of the Uniswap V2 USDT/USDC liquidity pool).

     2. Tokenized shares (TAF) in Apple (AAPL), Tesla (TSLA) and Facebook (FB).

Following the beta, we will allow other individuals to borrow against their positions in any oUSD to USDT/USDC liquidity pool. You can read more about oUSD and its role in bringing real world assets on-chain here.

What is the Asset Explorer?

The asset explorer is where you can view the tokenized assets being used as collateral on the system. It includes the token name, the type of asset, it’s collateral value on the system, and the link to the token contract itself. By clicking the link under asset Type, you will be directed to an information page with more details on the asset.

How often is interest calculated? Can I take my deployed funds out any time?

Interest is calculated per each ethereum block which is roughly every 15 seconds. You can take out your funds, including interest, anytime - provided there is enough liquidity in the pool. Liquidity of a particular asset is shown when you click the asset. In the example below there is currently 194.06 DAI which can be withdrawn from the pool.


I just deployed, and the interest rate went down. Why?

The interest rate from the pool is dynamically adjusted. As more lenders deploy, the interest will reduce until the additional amount in the pool is borrowed. This is similar to utilisation rate on other protocols. When more borrowers borrow from the pool interest rates will come back up.

Clicking Enable, Deploy or Withdraw doesn’t seem to do anything

Firstly, ensure you’re connected to the OPM with Metamask. Clicking any of the above buttons will require your wallet to confirm the transaction, which is a prompt you’ll have to confirm. Once you have confirmed the transaction the OPM will refresh to reflect your changes.

What are OPEN Governance Tokens?

The OPM is part of the OpenDAO ecosystem. Lenders, and eventually borrowers, will be rewarded with Open Governance Tokens for performing these actions. Token holders have a vote and say on which assets can be used and borrowed against on the OPM as well as other governance initiatives. You can learn more about OpenDAO and Open tokens here.

What are kTokens?

kTokens are similar to cTokens, or aTokens and iTokens etc from other money market platforms out there. They represent your claim to the money you have deployed on the OPM and the interest generated.

Once you deploy DAI to the platform you will receive kDAI in return. These are interest bearing tokens and steadily rise in value.

While on the platform interface they are not visible, you may check them in your metamask by adding those custom tokens.
