Frequently Asked Questions


What is the OTL?

Open Token Lending (OTL) is a censorship resistant money market protocol where lenders can get a consistent rate of return with real world collateral in a permissionless, trust minimized manner

How do I interact with the OTL?

You can connect via metamask or wallet connect to the OTL and supply DAI, USDC and USDT to the platform and start earning interest immediately. You may withdraw your deployed tokens at any time, including accrued interest (provided there is liquidity, which we are working towards improving).

What can I lend with?

Our goal is to make it possible to lend any token against any token, however right now we allow DAI, USDC and USDT. More currency pegged tokens will be added in the coming days on the lending side.

Who can borrow from the platform? What is being used as collateral?

Eventually anyone with tokenised assets can borrow from the platform by using the asset as collateral. Right now we have tokenized shares in Apple (AAPL), Tesla (TSLA) and Facebook (FB) and are using these stocks as collateral. You can check details of the collateral from the Asset Explorer and read why we chose listed shares as our first collateral here. These shares have a collateral factor of 80% of their value on the OTL.

What is the Asset Explorer?

The asset explorer is where you can view the tokenized assets being used as collateral on the system. It includes the token name, the type of asset, it’s collateral value on the system, and the link to the token contract itself. By clicking the link under asset Link, you will be directed to an information page with more details on the asset including any relevant loan agreements. In the case of the tokenised shares, it will show the publicly viewable portfolio of the shares on Etoro.


How often is interest calculated? Can I take my deployed funds out any time?

Interest is calculated per each ethererum block which is roughly every 15 seconds. You can take out your funds, including interest, anytime - provided there is enough liquidity in the pool. Liquidity of a particular asset is shown when you click the asset. In the example below there is currently 115.03 USDT which can be withdrawn from the pool.


I just deployed, and the interest rate went down. Why?

The interest rate from the pool is dynamically adjusted. As more lenders deploy, the interest will reduce until the additional amount in the pool is borrowed. This is similar to utilisation rate on other protocols. When more borrowers borrow from the pool interest rates will come back up.

Clicking Enable, Deploy or Withdraw doesn’t seem to do anything

Firstly, ensure you’re connected to the OTL with either Metamask or Wallet Connect. Clicking any of the above buttons will require your wallet to confirm the transaction, which is a prompt you’ll have to confirm. Once you have confirmed the transaction, the OTL will refresh to reflect your changes.

Are there governance tokens?

Once the alpha is complete, OTL platform governance tokens will be distributed to investors, core team and to the community. You can read more about our governance token plans here.

What are kTokens?

kTokens are similar to cTokens, or aTokens and iTokens etc from other money market platforms out there. They represent your claim to the money you have deployed on the platform and its interest.

Once you deploy DAI to the platform you will receive an ERC20 kDAI. This is interest bearing token and steadily rise in value.

While on the platform interface they are not visible, you may check them in your metamask by adding this custom token.
